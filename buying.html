WHICH VAN OR RV IS RIGHT FOR YOU?

We've assembled a quick guide to help narrow down your choices.

CARGO VANS

There are two types of cargo vans: passenger ones that have windows and seats, or panel ones that have no windows. They offer a lot of flexibility as far as height and length, and interior customizability.

  • LENGTH: ~14.5-16 ft
  • SLEEPS: 1-2
  • COST: $-$$
  • PROS: These come in a lot of shapes and sizes. Compact and extended length options are available, as well as hi-top roofs. Also easy to stealth camp.
  • CONS: Poor gas mileage on older models. No bathroom, unless you make room for a portable toilet.

CONVERSION VANS

Typically designed with road trips in mind, most have hi-top roofs for extra room and large windows already. If you're anxious to hit the road, most also have a bench seat that folds down into a bed in the back.

  • LENGTH: ~18 ft
  • SLEEPS: 1-2
  • COST: $-$$
  • PROS: Older models can have some interesting exterior colors and designs. Super comfy front seats.
  • CONS: Older models can have some interesting exterior colors and designs. The inside will most likely need to be gutted if you want a more modern look (which is also a pro if you like projects).

CLASS B MOTORHOMES

Also known as Camper Vans, these are the smallest RV class. For the base, most people use a Ram ProMaster, Mercedes-Benz Sprinter, or Ford Transit.

  • LENGTH: ~20 ft
  • SLEEPS: 1-6
  • COST: $$$ ($50,000-100,000 depending on year and condition)
  • PROS: Can fit a bathroom and/or shower, as well as sleeping space for additional people. Decent gas mileage depending on model (up to 25 mpg).
  • CONS: These are not cheap, and it's difficult to stealth camp. Poor gas mileage depending on model (as low as 10 mpg).

CLASS C MOTORHOMES

These are a great middle option between a Class B and A, for those that want more space but not drive a monster Class A.

  • LENGTH: ~28-40 ft
  • SLEEPS: 1-6
  • COST: $$$ ($80,000-150,000 depending on year and condition)
  • PROS: Can fit a bathroom and/or shower and has a lot more space.
  • CONS: Not stealthy and fairly expensive. With a bathroom comes a blackwater tank, which can be a hassle.

CLASS A MOTORHOMES

These are behemoths, similar in size to a bus. If you have a large family or like a lot of space, this is the perfect RV for you.

  • LENGTH: ~21-45 ft
  • SLEEPS: 1-10
  • COST: $$$$ ($100,000-500,000+ depending on year and condition)
  • PROS: The peak of luxury - can easily look like a high-end apartment on the inside. Lots of space: can fit a full-size fridge, bathroom and shower.
  • CONS: Very expensive. Difficult to stealth camp and parking needs extra consideration. Poor gas mileage ranging from 8-13 mpg.
